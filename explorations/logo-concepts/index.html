Logo directions — concepts to react to, not finals. Each mark shown as a 96px app icon, scaled down to a 16px menu-bar glyph, and locked up with the Doto wordmark. Cyan #39BFC6 on black; red #ff5454 reserved for the REC accent.
Design-lead read · ## Shortlist (strongest first) 1. **Waveform Roger** — The only un-copyable mark here; the pirate is yohoho's moat, and teeth=waveform / eye=REC is the densest double-read in the set. 2. **Dot Matrix Y** — The truest system glyph: it *is* the panel's LED grid, scales to a 16px favicon, and the foot-dot REC read is fully earned. Safe, foundational, quiet on personality. 3. **Five-Ho Voiceprint** — Purest column-dot/Doto DNA, and the only one that doubles as a wordmark underline and a natural *recording-state* animation. Weak as a standalone square, strong as a signature. 4. **Open Aperture (REC Core)** — Best of the ring/sonar family; the broken-open ring + escaping pings lifts it past a generic status light into something crafted. 5. **Message in a Bottle** — Most narratively pirate and genuinely ownable, but illustrative and risky at favicon scale (reads as potion/perfume; negative-space waveform vanishes small). A bit costume-y for "refined utilitarian." 6. **Three-Ho Bloom** — Clever idea, illegible execution: a viewer sees abstract rings, not a laugh. Redundant with Aperture. 7. **Sonar Sweep** — Competent but the least ownable thing you could ship. ## Push hardest: Waveform Roger You should develop the one mark only yohoho can own. Every competitor can make a waveform or a lens; nobody else is a pirate, and the name *demands* it. Its one real risk (costume-y / mush at small sizes) is fully solvable: **build the skull out of the LED dot-matrix grid** so it inherits the panel's DNA, the grid discipline, and the scalability — a smirk stitched in lights, not a sticker. ## Best combine: Dot Matrix Y (#1) + Five-Ho Voiceprint (#6) Make them one *living* icon instead of two logos. The **Y** is the at-rest app glyph; when recording, its dots resolve into the **voiceprint firing out of the red REC dot**, then settle back. Same grid, same red accent, one continuous identity that encodes the product's core action (rest → listen → record). That's "functional in more than one way" as motion, not just as a pun. ## Cut **Sonar Sweep** — radiating arcs in a rounded square is the single most over-used motif in voice/audio apps; no pirate, no dot-matrix DNA, nothing only-yohoho about it. (Three-Ho Bloom is the runner-up cut — keep it only if Aperture dies.)
